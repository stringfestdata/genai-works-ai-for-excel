# Community & Logistics — Slack Playbook

A live cohort lives or dies in the gaps between sessions. This is how to keep the
Slack space active and useful with light effort.

---

## Channel structure (keep it simple)

| Channel | Purpose |
|---|---|
| `#announcements` | Session times, links, recordings, deadlines. Instructor-posts-only. |
| `#general` | Intros, questions, discussion. The main room. |
| `#share-your-work` | Where the per-session "post your result" assignments land. |
| `#capstones` | Capstone work-in-progress, drafts, and final submissions. |
| `#help` (optional) | Tech/setup troubleshooting so it doesn't clog `#general`. |

If your platform limits channels, collapse to `#general`, `#share-your-work`, and
`#capstones`.

---

## Cadence (what to post, when)

**Before the cohort starts**
- Welcome post + the intro prompt (below).
- Pin: schedule, dataset/repo links, how to get help, the data-safety note.

**Day before each session**
- Reminder with time + join link + the session's dataset link.

**Right after each session**
- Recording link, the slides, and the session's assignment as its own thread in
  `#share-your-work`. Tag the deliverable clearly.

**Between sessions (the important part)**
- React to and reply to every "share your work" post. Visible instructor presence
  is what keeps people posting.
- Mid-week nudge: "Halfway to Session 2 — drop your cleaned-file screenshot if you
  haven't yet." Light, encouraging, specific.

**During week two (capstone week)**
- A daily-ish check-in prompt in `#capstones`.
- Offer one async "office hours" thread where people can post a screenshot and get
  unstuck.

## Copy-paste prompts

**Intro prompt (pin it):**
> 👋 Welcome! Drop a quick intro: (1) your role, (2) the Excel task you most want
> to stop doing by hand, and (3) what you're hoping to build by the end. We'll
> come back to #2 when you pick your capstone.

**After Session 1:**
> 📊 Session 1 assignment: clean `regional-sales-RAW.csv` in Power Query. Post a
> screenshot of your clean table's **row count + total Revenue** (aim ~248 rows,
> ≈ $555K) and **one sentence** on a decision you made. Thread below 👇

**After Session 2:**
> 🧮 Session 2 assignment: reproduce the department variance summary (any road —
> formula, Python, or Copilot) and post your chart/summary. Bonus: share a
> before/after of a prompt you improved. And tell us the recurring report you'll
> use for your capstone.

**After Session 3:**
> 🚀 Capstone time: build a workflow on your own (anonymized) data that refreshes
> with minimal manual work. Post your workbook + run-book + one sentence on where
> you still review the AI by hand. Deadline: `<date>`. Peer feedback welcome!

---

## Logistics checklist (per cohort)

- [ ] Repo / materials link shared and pinned (datasets, solutions, slides, cheat sheet, capstone brief)
- [ ] Recording workflow confirmed (live cohort doubles as the on-demand recording)
- [ ] Calendar invites sent with join links for all three sessions
- [ ] Data-safety note pinned (anonymize; keep regulated data out of public AI tools)
- [ ] Deadline for capstone set and announced
- [ ] Support contact shared: **Support@academy.genai.works**

---

## A note on keeping it generic

These materials are intentionally vendor-neutral and brand-light so they slot into
the GenAI Works Academy environment cleanly. Keep promotional/self-referential
content out of the cohort space per platform guidelines — the value you provide is
the teaching and the responsiveness.
